Counterfactual Sentence Recognition Task


Instruction:


You will be presented with 20 sentences, and most of them are counterfactual sentences. Counterfactual sentences usually consist of two parts, an antecedent and a corresponding consequent. The antecedent has the assumption that is critical for a counterfactual statement, while the consequent is the result of what will happen assuming the assumption which is counter to the facts is true. However, not all the counterfactual sentences have consequents, as consequents can be implied. Be careful. For every counterfactual sentence, the antecedent part is essential but consequent part can be optional. Also, the order of antecedent and consequent in the sentence can be arbitary. Thus, you need to be careful to select properly.


For every counterfactual sentence with both an antecedent and consequent, it is possible to rewrite them into a simple sentence with the same meaning showing the causal-and-effect relationship between the antecedent and consequent. Thus, for the following work you need to first figure out whether it is a counterfactual. If it isn¡¯t, please tick the checkbox and move to next sentence. If it is, then please extract the antecedent and the consequent from the original sentence using copy and paste and then convert the counterfactual sentence into a free-form sentence showing the relationship.Try not to copy and paste irrelevant information when extracting the antecedents and consequents. If there is no consequent , just leave the consequent and rewrite part blank. The examples below can show the detail of the task.


Here are some examples:

  1. Her post-traumatic stress could have been avoided if a combination of paroxetine and exposure therapy had been prescribed two months earlier.

    Antecedent: if a combination of paroxetine and exposure therapy had been prescribed two months earlier

    Consequent: Her post-traumatic stress could have been avoided

    Rewritten sentence: A combination of paroxetine and exposure therapy is useful for treating post-traumatic stress.

  2. I wish I could have seen Esteban there or have done something differently there.

    Antecedent: I wish I could have seen Esteban there or have done something differently there.

  3. Had the researchers tested their "vitamin mix" in pill form or mixed with water, it might have shown that blood sugar got better," says Buse.

    Antecedent: Had the researchers tested their "vitamin mix" in pill form or mixed with water

    Consequent: it might have shown that blood sugar got better

    Rewritten sentence: "Vitamin mix" in pill form or mixed with water may have better performance on adjusting blood sugar.

  4. The investigators estimated that if quarterly increases in male unemployment had been constrained over the 23 years, 188 very low weight births could have been prevented in Norway and approximately 329 in Sweden.

    Antecedent: if quarterly increases in male unemployment had been constrained over the 23 years

    Consequent: 188 very low weight births could have been prevented in Norway and approximately 329 in Sweden

    Rewrite sentence: Male unemployment could lead to low weight births.
