Counterfactual Sentence Rewriting Task


Instruction:


For this task you will be given 10 sentences and 3 random words for each sentence. Your job is to find a way to naturally incorporate only ONE of these words in the sentence without changing its counterfactual nature or its general meaning. It is much preferred if you do not remove or move around anything in the sentence but merely add an extra clause or an extra part that contains the word and is consistent with the context of the sentence. Do not write a new sentence, the word must be contained within the one sentence!


Half of the sentences you will be given will need to incorporate future based words, and the other half will have to incorporate past-based words. For the future based words, you should find a way to talk about something related to the future in the sentence based on the context of the sentence. For the past based words, you should talk about or expand some information about a fact that is established somewhere in the sentence.


Do not lazily put the word somewhere in the sentence without any thought. The sentence still has to be grammatically correct and make sense when you read it. If it doesn¡¯t, think about how else to incorporate it, or use a different word.


TIPS:

Its okay to make the sentence long, in fact it is recommended, provided that you find a natural way to incorporate the word in the sentence.

Try to talk about some future based scenario relating to the facts of the sentence for the future based words as an aside, it will make it easier to incorporate these words in the sentence, and it is inline with the goal of the task to make tricky sentences where it is difficult to detect whether the sentence is about the past or the future. Likewise, do something similar in the past case too except try to make it clearly factual. This would make it harder to detect whether it is factual or counterfactual.

  • Original Sentence: If I had seen him, I would have told you about it
  • Words/Phrases to Include: coming times, approaching, potentially
  • Modified Sentence: If I had seen him and in the coming times, I hope that I will, I would have told you about it

Out of the 3 words given, coming times was chosen. Notice how the part added with the phrase doesn¡¯t make the sentence non-counterfactual but still discusses some future events related to the context of the sentence. The original meaning of the sentence hasn¡¯t been changed; it has only been slightly expanded. This is what we want.
